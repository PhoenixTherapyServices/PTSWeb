Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer telehealth or in-person sessions?

Yes, we offer both telehealth and in-person sessions to accommodate your needs.

Do you take insurance?

Yes, we accept many insurance providers. Please contact us to verify your coverage.

Do you do individual sessions?

Yes, individual therapy sessions are available for all ages.

Do you do family sessions?

Yes, we provide family therapy to support communication and relationships.

Can you accommodate multiple schedules?

We offer flexible scheduling to help meet the needs of different family members and individuals.

Do you treat ADHD?

Yes, we provide assessment and therapy for ADHD for both children and adults.

Do you provide medication?

We do not prescribe medication, but we work closely with prescribing providers when necessary.

Is mental health a medical condition?

Yes, mental health conditions are recognized medical conditions that affect emotional, psychological, and social well-being.

What do you use to diagnose mental health symptoms?

We use standardized assessments, clinical interviews, and observation to evaluate mental health symptoms.

Do you work with PCPs?

Yes, we collaborate with primary care providers when appropriate to ensure integrated care.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes, confidentiality is a core part of therapy, with a few legal exceptions for safety purposes.

Is mental health therapy different than counseling?

Therapy and counseling often overlap; therapy may be longer-term and address deeper patterns, while counseling may focus on short-term guidance and support.

How long is a therapy session?

Standard sessions are typically 50-60 minutes, but may vary depending on your needs.

What is the shortest session I can attend and still use my insurance?

Insurance usually covers standard-length sessions; please check with your provider to confirm minimum requirements.

Can I see more than 1 therapist at a time?

Typically, clients work with one therapist at a time, but we coordinate care if multiple providers are needed.

What is EMDR?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a therapy used to help process trauma and distressing memories.

What is an eating disorder?

Eating disorders are mental health conditions that affect eating behaviors and attitudes toward food and body image.

How do you treat eating disorders?

We provide individualized treatment including therapy, nutritional guidance, and coordinated care to support recovery.
